Biocuration has become a cornerstone for analyses in biology, and to meet needs, 
the amount of annotations has considerably grown in recent years. However, the 
reliability of these annotations varies; it has thus become necessary to be able 
to assess the confidence in annotations. Although several resources already 
provide confidence information about the annotations that they produce, a 
standard way of providing such information has yet to be defined. This lack of 
standardization undermines the propagation of knowledge across resources, as 
well as the credibility of results from high-throughput analyses. Seeded at a 
workshop during the Biocuration 2012 conference, a working group has been 
created to address this problem. We present here the elements that were 
identified as essential for assessing confidence in annotations, as well as a 
draft ontology--the Confidence Information Ontology--to illustrate how the 
problems identified could be addressed. We hope that this effort will provide a 
home for discussing this major issue among the biocuration community. Tracker
