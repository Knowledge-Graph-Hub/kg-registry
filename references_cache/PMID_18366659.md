BACKGROUND: Dictyostelium discoideum is a model system for studying many 
important physiological processes including chemotaxis, phagocytosis, and signal 
transduction. The recent sequencing of the genome has revealed the presence of 
over 12,500 protein-coding genes. The model organism database dictyBase hosts 
the genome sequence as well as a large amount of manually curated information.
RESULTS: We present here an anatomy ontology for Dictyostelium based upon the 
life cycle of the organism.
CONCLUSION: Anatomy ontologies are necessary to annotate species-specific events 
such as phenotypes, and the Dictyostelium anatomy ontology provides an essential 
tool for curation of the Dictyostelium genome.
