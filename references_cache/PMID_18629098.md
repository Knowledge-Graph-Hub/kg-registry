We are endowed with a rich knowledge about Caenorhabditis elegans. Its 
stereotyped anatomy and development has stimulated research and resulted in the 
accumulation of cell-based information concerning gene expression, and the role 
of specific cells in developmental signalling and behavioural circuits. To make 
the information more accessible to sophisticated queries and automated retrieval 
systems, WormBase has begun to construct a C. elegans cell and anatomy ontology. 
Here we present our strategies and progress.
