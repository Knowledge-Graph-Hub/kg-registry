# National Organization for Rare Disorders

## Overview

The National Organization for Rare Disorders (NORD) is a unique patient advocacy organization dedicated to individuals with rare diseases and the organizations that serve them. Founded in 1983, NORD provides crucial services including comprehensive disease information, patient assistance programs, research funding, and policy advocacy for the rare disease community.

## Rare Disease Database

### Content Coverage
- **1,200+ Disease Reports**: Comprehensive information on rare diseases
- **Patient-Friendly Language**: Written for patients, families, and caregivers
- **Medical Professional Resources**: Detailed clinical information
- **Regular Updates**: Continuously updated with latest research and treatment information

### Information Provided
- Disease descriptions and synonyms
- Signs and symptoms
- Causes and inheritance patterns
- Affected populations and demographics
- Diagnosis and testing information
- Treatment and therapies
- Clinical trials information
- Resources and support organizations
- References to medical literature

## Key Programs & Services

### Patient Assistance Programs
- **Medication Assistance**: Help accessing medications
- **Diagnostic Assistance**: Support for diagnostic testing
- **Travel Assistance**: Help getting to specialized care

### Research Support
- **Research Grants**: Funding for rare disease research
- **Research Pipeline**: Connecting researchers with resources

### Advocacy & Policy
- **Orphan Drug Act**: Instrumental in passing landmark legislation
- **Policy Advocacy**: Fighting for rare disease patients' rights

- **International Collaboration**: Global rare disease initiatives

### Education & Outreach
- **Rare Disease Day**: Annual awareness campaign

## Definition of Rare Disease

In the U.S., a rare disease is defined as a condition affecting fewer than 200,000 Americans. There are approximately 7,000 known rare diseases affecting an estimated 25-30 million Americans.
