# CDC/ATSDR Social Vulnerability Index

## Overview

The CDC/ATSDR Social Vulnerability Index (SVI) ranks the relative social
vulnerability of U.S. communities to external stresses on human health, such as
natural disasters and disease outbreaks. It draws on 16 U.S. Census variables
organized into four themes — socioeconomic status, household characteristics,
racial and ethnic minority status, and housing type and transportation — and
summarizes them into vulnerability scores at the census-tract and county level.

The index is maintained by the CDC and the Agency for Toxic Substances and
Disease Registry (ATSDR) through their Geospatial Research, Analysis, and
Services Program (GRASP).

## Access

- Data and documentation: download SVI datasets (CSV and geospatial formats)
  and accompanying documentation for multiple years from 2000 through 2022.
- Interactive map: explore SVI scores and underlying census variables by census
  tract and county through a web-based map.

## Related resources

The SVI serves as an upstream source for the KnowWhereGraph.

## Terms and citation

The SVI is produced by CDC/ATSDR, U.S. federal agencies; its data are generally
in the U.S. public domain. Please attribute the CDC/ATSDR Geospatial Research,
Analysis, and Services Program (GRASP) when using the index.
