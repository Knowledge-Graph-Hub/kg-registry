# SRPDB

SRPDB (Signal Recognition Particle Database) is a specialized database dedicated to signal recognition particles (SRP), providing comprehensive information about the structure and function of these essential ribonucleoprotein complexes involved in protein translocation.

## Overview

Signal recognition particles are universally conserved ribonucleoprotein complexes that play a crucial role in targeting nascent proteins to cellular membranes. SRPDB assists researchers in studying SRP by providing carefully curated, aligned, and phylogenetically organized sequences of both SRP RNA and protein components.

## Database Contents

### SRP RNA Sequences

SRPDB contains a total of 1,109 SRP RNA sequences organized into seven major phylogenetic groups:

- **Short Bacterial (4.5S)**: 537 sequences
- **Long Bacterial (6S)**: 96 sequences
- **Archaeal**: 81 sequences
- **Protozoan**: 82 sequences
- **Fungal/Metazoan-group**: 80 sequences
- **Plant**: 114 sequences
- **Animal**: 119 sequences

### SRP Proteins

The database includes aligned and annotated sequences for all major SRP protein components:

- **Core SRP Proteins**: SRP9, SRP14, SRP19, SRP21, SRP54, SRP68, SRP72
- **Chloroplast SRP**: cpSRP43
- **SRP Receptors**: Alpha subunit (FtsY), Beta subunit
- **Related Proteins**: Flhf, CaM kinase II

## Key Features

### Multiple Sequence Alignments
All sequences are carefully aligned and can be browsed both phylogenetically and alphabetically, facilitating comparative studies across different organisms and taxonomic groups.

### Secondary Structure Diagrams
Representative RNA secondary structure diagrams are provided, with each base pair validated through comparative sequence analysis. This ensures that the proposed structures are supported by evolutionary evidence.

### Structural Information
- **2D Structures**: Secondary structure diagrams with conserved motifs
- **3D Structures**: Experimentally determined three-dimensional structures
- **Models**: Computational models of SRP components

### Phylogenetic Organization
Sequences are organized phylogenetically, allowing researchers to track evolutionary relationships and conservation patterns across different domains of life.

## Historical Significance

The database documents the history of SRP research, including:
- Key publication from 1972
- Commentary from 1999 Nobel Prize winner Günter Blobel
- Connection to the 1999 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine

## Methodology

### Comparative Sequence Analysis
Base pairs in secondary structure diagrams are validated using comparative sequence analysis, a powerful approach that identifies conserved structural elements based on covariation in aligned sequences.

### Annotation
All sequences are manually annotated and curated, with special attention to:
- Conserved sequence motifs
- Functional domains
- Phylogenetic relationships
- Structural features
