# Agricultural Collaborative Research Outcomes System (AgCROS)

## Overview

The Agricultural Collaborative Research Outcomes System (AgCROS) is a growing "network
of networks" operated by the USDA Agricultural Research Service (USDA-ARS). It brings
together several agricultural research data networks under a single point of access
to help researchers and stakeholders discover, use, and share agricultural science
data.

## Data Content

AgCROS member networks include, among others:

- **GRACEnet** — Greenhouse gas Reduction through Agricultural Carbon Enhancement network
- **NUOnet** — Nutrient Uptake and Outcomes Network
- **REAP** — Resilient Economic Agricultural Practices
- **LTAR** — Long-Term Agroecosystem Research Network
- **SHAnet** — Soil Health Assessment Network

The data span soil physical, chemical, and biological properties, greenhouse gas
emissions, crop yields, and management practices collected from long-term
experimental fields.

## Use in Knowledge Graphs

AgCROS is the primary upstream data source for the Soil Organic Carbon Knowledge
Graph (SOCKG), which integrates and maps AgCROS experimental data spanning dozens of
experimental fields over several decades.
