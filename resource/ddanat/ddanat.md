# Dictyostelium discoideum anatomy

## Overview

The Dictyostelium discoideum anatomy ontology (ddanat) is a structured controlled vocabulary for describing the anatomy of the slime-mold *Dictyostelium discoideum*. This ontology provides standardized terms for anatomical structures and developmental stages of this important model organism used in cell biology, developmental biology, and genetics research.

## Scope and Coverage

The ontology covers:

- **Anatomical structures**: Cell types, organelles, and multicellular structures
- **Developmental stages**: From single-cell amoeba through aggregation to fruiting body
- **Morphological features**: Specific anatomical characteristics at different life stages
- **Spatial relationships**: Anatomical part-of and other spatial relations

## Key Features

- **OBO Foundry member**: Follows OBO Foundry principles for interoperability
- **Rich axiomatization**: Formal logical definitions for automated reasoning
- **Cross-references**: Links to other relevant ontologies and databases
- **Species-specific**: Focused on *Dictyostelium discoideum* (NCBITaxon:44689)

## Use Cases

- Annotating gene expression data from dictyBase
- Describing phenotypes in genetic studies
- Standardizing anatomical descriptions in publications
- Enabling computational analysis of developmental processes
- Supporting comparative anatomy studies
