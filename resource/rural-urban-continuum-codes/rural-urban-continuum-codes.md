## Description

The **Rural-Urban Continuum Codes (RUCC)** are a county-level classification
scheme maintained by the **USDA Economic Research Service (ERS)**. They sort US
counties into a continuum that distinguishes metropolitan from nonmetropolitan
status and captures the degree of urbanization. Metro counties are grouped by
the population size of their metro area, while nonmetro counties are
distinguished by degree of urbanization and whether they are adjacent to a
metro area.

RUCC is a long-standing, widely adopted measure of rurality used in
socioeconomic and geographic research. In the KG-Registry it is recorded as an
upstream source of the **Cancer Registry KG**, where it supports socioeconomic
and geographic linkage of county-level data.
