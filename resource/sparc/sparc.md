# Stimulating Peripheral Activity to Relieve Conditions (SPARC)

## Overview

Stimulating Peripheral Activity to Relieve Conditions (SPARC) is an NIH Common Fund
program that accelerates development of therapeutic devices that modulate electrical
activity in nerves to improve organ function. This therapeutic strategy, known as
bioelectronic medicine, offers potential new treatment options for diverse diseases
and conditions such as hypertension, heart failure, chronic pain, and
gastrointestinal disorders.

## The SPARC Portal

The SPARC Portal (sparc.science) is a public resource created to share information
and tools on the anatomy and function of the autonomic and peripheral nervous
system. It provides access to digital resources including data sets, anatomical and
functional maps, and predictive simulations that can be shared, cited, visualized,
computed on, and used for virtual experimentation.

## Key Features

- **Curated Data Sets**: Structure and function data on the peripheral nervous
  system and its connections to internal organs
- **Anatomical Maps**: Connectivity maps among nerves and organ systems
- **Computational Models**: Predictive simulations for bioelectronic medicine
- **Open Access**: Data shared under a CC BY 4.0 license

## Products

### SPARC Portal
Web portal for browsing, visualizing, and downloading SPARC data sets, maps, and

### SPARC Data Sets
